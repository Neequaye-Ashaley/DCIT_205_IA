SCHOOL OF PHYSICAL AND MATHEMATICAL SCIENCES ORGANIZES A SEND-OFF FOR RETIREES AND FORMER HEADS OF DEPARTMENT The School of Physical and Mathematical Sciences (SPMS) has organized a send-off for staff who retired from the service of the University on July 31, 2023, and Heads of Department whose tenure ended on July 31, 2023. The purpose was to acknowledge their hard work and to formally appreciate them for their tremendous efforts and contributions towards attainment of the objectives of the School, and the University as a whole.

SCHOOL OF PHYSICAL AND MATHEMATICAL SCIENCES ORGANISES A PUBLIC ENGAGEMENT ON THE DISCOVERY OF HIV POLY PROTEIN STRUCTURE The School of Physical and Mathematical Sciences (SPMS) organized a public engagement on the topic, ‘Chasing an enigma: unmasking the mystery of HIV polyproteins for potential drug development.’ This follows from a cutting-edge research undertaken by Dr. Jerry Joe Harrison, a Senior Lecturer in the Department of Chemistry and his collaborators from Rutgers University and Salk Institute in the USA.

SCHOOL OF PHYSICAL AND MATHEMATICAL SCIENCES HELD A TRAINING PROGRAMME FOR ADMINISTRATIVE STAFF The School of Physical and Mathematical Sciences (SPMS) organized a training program for administrative staff of the School. The purpose of the training was to equip the staff with requisite skills to undertake their duties effectively. In his opening remarks, the Dean of SPMS, Prof. Sandow Mark Yidana indicated that for staff to play their supportive roles effectively, the School needed to provide them with the necessary skills. This would enable them contribute towards the attainment of the School and the University’s overall objectives. He expressed gratitude to the School Administrator for putting the program together, and was thankful to participants for the positive response. He was hopeful that more training would be organized for other categories of staff. Welcoming the participants, the School Administrator, Ms. Mawuena Abortta expressed her delight at the interest shown by the participants. The training was facilitated by Mr. Michael Addo-Boateng, Archivist, University of Ghana Archives and Dr. Yvonne Lamptey, Senior Lecturer, Department of Organization and Human Resource Management, University of Ghana Business School. The Facilitators gave presentations on Records Management, Business Communication, and Office Management.

The School of Physical and Mathematical Sciences (SPMS) Celebrated ‘Day of Scientific Renaissance of Africa’ by organizing a seminar and exhibition of scholarly works on Wednesday, 29th June, 2022. The seminar, which was held at the Science Conference Room, Daniel Adjei Bekoe Building, was delivered by Dr. Isaac Wiafe, a Senior Lecturer in the Department of Computer Science and it was on the topic Designing Persuasive Technologies: Current State, Prospects and Challenges. The program attracted about 140 participants including faculty members, staff and students from different units of the University. It was moderated by Mr. Michael Agbo Soli.
